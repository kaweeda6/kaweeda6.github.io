*"Every six months."* You have heard it your entire life. But where did that guideline actually come from — and is it right for you?

The twice-yearly recommendation traces back to a 1950s Pepsodent toothpaste marketing campaign, not a clinical study. The real answer is more nuanced, and it depends entirely on the state of your oral health.

## The Three Cleaning Schedules

### Every 6 Months — For Low-Risk Patients

Twice-yearly cleanings are appropriate if you:
- Have a consistent home care routine (brushing twice daily, flossing daily)
- Have no history of gum disease
- Have minimal plaque and tartar buildup
- Have no systemic conditions that affect oral health

For these patients, six-month intervals are sufficient to remove the buildup that daily brushing misses and to catch any early changes before they become problems.

### Every 3–4 Months — For Moderate to High-Risk Patients

More frequent cleanings are recommended if you:
- Have a history of gum disease (periodontitis), even if treated
- Are undergoing active periodontal therapy
- Have a high cavity rate or deep grooves in your teeth
- Smoke or use tobacco products
- Have dry mouth from medications, radiation, or other causes
- Have orthodontic appliances, bridges, or implants that trap plaque

Once you have had gum disease, the bacteria that cause it do not disappear — they just become harder to manage. Quarterly "periodontal maintenance" cleanings are clinically proven to significantly reduce the recurrence of gum disease and the associated bone loss.

### Annually — Rarely, But It Exists

Some studies suggest that very-low-risk patients with exceptional oral hygiene may only need one cleaning per year. This requires a careful clinical assessment and is typically a status you reach after years of excellent hygiene and clean checkups.

## What Actually Happens at a Cleaning?

A professional cleaning at Thrive Dental includes:

1. **Scaling** — removing calculus (hardened tartar) above and below the gumline using hand scalers and ultrasonic instruments
2. **Polishing** — removing surface stains and soft plaque with a slightly abrasive paste
3. **Flossing** — cleaning between teeth to remove any remaining debris
4. **Exam** — your dentist reviews X-rays, checks for cavities, evaluates gum health, and screens for oral cancer

For patients with gum disease, a deeper procedure called **scaling and root planing** (deep cleaning) may be performed under local anesthesia to clean below the gumline where a regular cleaning cannot reach.

## Why Skipping Cleanings Costs More

Here is the math patients often miss:

- A routine cleaning costs **$100–$250** (often fully covered by insurance)
- A deep cleaning (scaling and root planing) costs **$600–$1,200** per quadrant
- A filling costs **$150–$400** per tooth
- A root canal and crown costs **$2,000–$3,500** per tooth

Every year you skip a cleaning, you increase the probability of needing that more expensive downstream care. We have seen patients return after a 3-year gap who needed thousands of dollars of work that could have been prevented by a couple of routine cleanings.

**Most adults: twice yearly.** If you have gum disease or significant risk factors: every 3–4 months. The right schedule is the one that keeps your mouth healthy — and that is different for everyone.
