Straightening your teeth in 2026 is better than ever — and the two most popular options are clear Invisalign aligners and traditional metal or ceramic braces. If you're weighing the decision, here's everything you need to know to choose the option that fits your life.

## The Short Answer

**Choose Invisalign if:** You're an adult or older teen who values aesthetics, wants removable aligners, and can commit to wearing them 20–22 hours per day.

**Choose braces if:** You have a complex bite issue, a younger teen who might forget to wear aligners, or you're looking for the lowest upfront cost.

## Appearance

This is where Invisalign wins decisively. The clear, custom-fit trays are virtually invisible — most people won't notice you're wearing them at all. Traditional metal braces are highly visible, though tooth-colored ceramic braces are a middle-ground option.

For adult professionals, clear aligners are often the deciding factor.

## Effectiveness: What Can Each Treat?

**Invisalign** handles most common alignment issues very well:
- Mild to moderate crowding
- Spacing between teeth
- Overbite, underbite, and crossbite (in many cases)
- Minor relapse after previous braces

**Traditional braces** have advantages for:
- Severe crowding or complex bite corrections
- Cases requiring precise vertical tooth movement
- Certain skeletal jaw corrections (often combined with orthodontic appliances)

As an **Invisalign Gold Plus Provider**, Dr. Mikhaylova treats the vast majority of adult and teen cases with clear aligners. We use iTero digital scanning to simulate your result before you even begin.

## Timeline

| Concern | Invisalign | Braces |
|---|---|---|
| Mild crowding | 6–12 months | 12–18 months |
| Moderate issues | 12–18 months | 18–24 months |
| Complex cases | 18–24 months | 24–36 months |

Invisalign often moves faster for straightforward cases because aligners apply gentle, continuous pressure. Complex bite issues may still resolve faster with braces.

## The Compliance Factor

Here's the honest truth: **Invisalign only works if you wear it.** The aligners must be in your mouth for 20–22 hours per day. You take them out to eat and drink (anything other than water) and to brush and floss.

For adults, this is usually straightforward. For younger teens, it can be a challenge — which is why Invisalign Teen includes compliance indicators and replacement aligners.

## Comfort

Invisalign trays are smooth plastic and cause less initial irritation to your cheeks and tongue than metal brackets and wires. Both systems cause some soreness when teeth are actively moving — typically the first few days after switching to a new tray or getting an adjustment.

## Oral Hygiene

Invisalign makes brushing and flossing significantly easier since the aligners come out completely. Patients with braces have higher rates of white spot lesions (early decay) from inadequate cleaning around brackets and wires.

## What Our Bayside Patients Choose

In our practice, roughly **70% of adult orthodontic patients choose Invisalign**. The aesthetics, convenience of eating whatever you want, and ability to take aligners out for special occasions make it the preferred option for most working adults.

For teens and complex cases, we discuss both options thoroughly and recommend what's clinically most appropriate.

## Retainers After Treatment: What Both Options Require

This part often surprises people: **both Invisalign and braces require retainers after treatment — for life.** Teeth have a natural tendency to shift back toward their original positions, especially in the first year post-treatment. Without retainers, relapse is nearly universal.

**After Invisalign:** You will wear your final set of aligners as retainers, or switch to a set of Vivera retainers (made by Invisalign, more durable). Full-time wear for 6–12 months, then nightly wear indefinitely.

**After braces:** A fixed retainer (a thin wire bonded behind the front teeth) is often placed immediately. A removable Hawley or clear retainer is worn at night.

The commitment to retainers is not optional — it is the final step that preserves the result you worked and paid for. We include retainer guidance in every orthodontic case we complete.

## Invisalign for Teens: What's Different

Standard Invisalign is designed for adults. Invisalign Teen addresses the specific challenges of treating younger patients:

- **Compliance indicators** — small blue dots on the aligners fade with wear. If the dots are still visible at a check-up, the patient is not wearing them enough.
- **Eruption compensation** — extra space built in for teeth that have not fully erupted yet (a common situation in 13–16 year olds).
- **Replacement aligners** — up to 6 replacement aligners included at no charge, because teenagers lose and damage trays.
- **More frequent check-ins** — we monitor teen cases more closely than adult cases.

For motivated teens who understand the commitment, Invisalign Teen produces beautiful results. For teens who are not yet ready to take responsibility for daily wear, traditional braces may be the more reliable choice — and that is an honest conversation we have with every family.

## Common Questions We Hear

**Does Invisalign hurt?**
Most patients experience mild pressure for 2–3 days after switching to a new tray. This is normal and a sign the aligners are working. Over-the-counter ibuprofen is usually sufficient.

**Can I eat whatever I want with Invisalign?**
Yes — you remove the trays to eat, so there are no food restrictions. This is a significant advantage over braces, where hard, sticky, and chewy foods can damage brackets and wires.

**Will Invisalign affect my speech?**
Some patients notice a slight lisp for the first few days while their tongue adjusts. This resolves quickly for most people.

**What if I lose a tray?**
Contact us immediately. In most cases, we will advise you to wear the previous tray while we order a replacement. There is typically a small fee for replacement trays outside of the Invisalign Teen plan.

**Is Invisalign faster than braces?**
For mild to moderate cases, yes — often by several months. For complex cases, the timelines are comparable or braces may be faster.
